# Gray Thoron, PhD Student
I'm Gray, a 4th year Physics PhD student at the University of Washington in Seattle. As part of [Nora Shipp's](https://norashipp.com/) research group, I study the disruption of satellite galaxies in cosmological simulations. I am interested in dark matter and low mass galaxies and out of equilibrium physics.

I recieved my B.S. in Physics (Astrophysics) from UC Santa Cruz. There, I worked with Tesla Jeltema on detecting offsets between the baryonic and dark matter centers of mass in post-merger galaxy clusters.

Beyond astrophysics, I play tabletop roleplaying games, mentor undergraduate storytellers and game designers in the UW Pen and Paper Gaming Association, bake and cook for friends and family, and enjoy hiking and ballroom dance.
